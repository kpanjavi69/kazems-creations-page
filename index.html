01 Book Masterclass

The Tactical Blueprint for Platform Mastery

Most weightlifting books only teach you how to train inside the gym. They focus entirely on sets, reps, and generic templates. But thousands of lifters train perfectly for months, only to fail completely when they step onto the competitive stage. They lose because they do not understand how to navigate the intense pressure, strict timing, and psychological rules of a real competition.

Six Attempts: Six White Lights is entirely different. Written from the multi-dimensional perspective of a Barcelona '92 Olympian, a Great Britain national team coach, a national record holder, and an elite International Technical Official, this book bridges the massive gap between gym strength and official platform success. It is the world's first comprehensive tactical guide to winning when the clock is ticking and three referees are watching your every move.

What You Will Master Inside:

  • The Science of the Warm-Up Room: Stop guessing when to take your last warm-up sets. Learn the exact timing protocols used by international coaches to ensure you step onto the platform perfectly peaked, warm, and confident—never rushed or cold.
  • Strategic Attempt Selection: Discover how to play the tactical card game of weightlifting. Learn how to open safely, build momentum, make smart weight jumps based on what your opponents are doing, and force competitors into mistakes.
  • Navigating the Technical Rulebook: Written by an active international referee, this guide pulls back the curtain on how officials judge lifts. Learn exactly what triggers a "red light" for press-outs, oscillations, or platform infractions, allowing you to clean up your technical habits.
  • Conquering Platform Nerves: Eliminate the choking, anxiety, and mental errors that ruin peak performance. Turn the chaos of the competitive stage into a predictable, practiced skill.

02 Book Masterclass

The Science of Female Athletic Longevity

Historically, sports science has ignored the female athlete. Almost every major strength training manual or programming textbook on the market is built entirely on research conducted on men. Coaches then blindly apply these male-centric templates to female lifters, leading to performance stagnation, unexplained fatigue, and a significantly higher rate of severe soft-tissue and joint injuries.

Female Olympic Weightlifters shatters this outdated paradigm. Written from the multi-dimensional perspective of a Barcelona '92 Olympian, a Great Britain national team coach, and a national record holder, this book is the culmination of a groundbreaking, multi-year study that tracked 100 female lifters across every competitive performance level. It provides coaches and athletes with a definitive, scientifically grounded blueprint to maximize strength, optimize health, and protect structural longevity.

What You Will Master Inside:

  • The 100-Athlete Empirical Dataset: Explore the real-world findings from a multi-year experiment documenting exactly how female bodies adapt, recover, and express power under heavy barbell loads.
  • True Hormonal Programming Synchronization: Stop treating recovery as a static variable. Learn how to accurately track shifting hormonal phases and structurally modify training block volume and intensity. Discover exactly when a female lifter's biology is optimized for maximum power expression versus when structural joints require strict protection.
  • Elite Advanced Recovery Protocols: Master the hidden variables of elite performance. Get comprehensive, evidence-based breakdowns detailing safe supplementation, precise hydration strategies, responsible medicine use, and targeted joint-preservation techniques.
  • Preventing the Gender Injury Gap: Understand the biomechanical and physiological markers that put female lifters at risk, and implement proactive coaching strategies to completely eliminate avoidable training setbacks.

06 Software

Rehearse the Match Before You Ever Step On the Platform

The lifting is only half the battle. Competitions are won and lost in the warm-up room and in the split-second decisions a coach makes under pressure—which weight to call, when to send the athlete out, how to react when a rival changes their attempt. Get those calls wrong and you can waste a lifter's entire preparation in a single afternoon. Too many coaches only learn these skills by making expensive mistakes on real competition day, at the cost of their athletes' results and confidence.

Kaz Competition IQ removes the guesswork. It is a full virtual competition simulator that recreates a real international tournament inside your own club—the same countdown clocks, lifting order, and referee light system used on the official platform—so coaches and athletes can practice the tactical, high-pressure side of the sport as many times as they need, long before it counts.

What the Software Lets You Do:

  • Simulate Real Match Scenarios: Run complete competitions in your home club and practice against realistic lifting orders, so match day feels familiar instead of chaotic.
  • Manage the Clock Like an Official: Countdown timers, lifting order, and referee light displays behave exactly like an international tournament, training you to work within the real time constraints.
  • Sharpen Coaching Decisions: Rehearse attempt calls, warm-up room timing, and weight selection under pressure. Practice reading opponents' moves and tactics so your decisions become trained instinct—not competition-day guesswork.
  • Build Mental Resilience: Prepare lifters for the nerves, the clock, and the chaotic stage environment, so pressure becomes something they have already rehearsed and mastered.

The Software That Completes the Book

Kaz Competition IQ is the living, interactive version of the tactics taught in Six Attempts: Six White Lights. Everything the book explains about attempt strategy, warm-up timing, and referee standards is turned into a hands-on simulator you can drill again and again. Together, the book gives you the knowledge and the software gives you the reps—so no coach is ever calling weights or sending an athlete to the platform for the very first time when it actually matters.

The Kaz Path System

Real-Time Precision, Where It Matters Most

Weightlifting technique is precise. Your feedback should be too.

Olympic weightlifting (Snatch, Clean, and Jerk) is a highly technical sport where small visual errors can lead to missed lifts and inconsistent development. Coaches rely heavily on visual judgment, and athletes often struggle to understand their movement errors until after the lift, via delayed video analysis — a gap that slows down the learning curve.

Introducing the Kaz Path System

Developed by Kazem Panjavi, an Olympian and experienced weightlifting coach with over 35 years of experience training 1,300+ athletes, this system provides simple, instant, real-time feedback inside the training environment, during the movement.

How It Works

Instead of relying on distracting screens, mobile apps, or delayed analysis, the Kaz Path System introduces a visual reference directly into the rack/platform area.

  • Simple & Instant: Immediate understanding of correct vs. incorrect movement.
  • Integrated: A practical coaching tool built into standard equipment.
  • Effective: Improves focus by reducing overthinking during the session.

The Lights

  • Star Lift — an exceptional, textbook rep.
  • Good Lift — clean and correct.
  • Not Bad — close, minor correction needed.
  • Wrong — technical error, adjust immediately.

Why You Need It

  • Fix Errors Instantly: Stops pushing forward or cutting your pull short.
  • Accelerate Learning: Builds perfect muscle memory in half the time.
  • Build Confidence: Gives you a physical, visual target to chase.
