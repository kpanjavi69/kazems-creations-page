The Kaz Path System

Real-Time Precision, Where It Matters Most

Weightlifting technique is precise. Your feedback should be too.

Olympic weightlifting (Snatch, Clean, and Jerk) is a highly technical sport where small visual errors can lead to missed lifts and inconsistent development. Coaches rely heavily on visual judgment, and athletes often struggle to understand their movement errors until after the lift, via delayed video analysis — a gap that slows down the learning curve.

Introducing the Kaz Path System

Developed by Kazem Panjavi, an Olympian and experienced weightlifting coach with over 35 years of experience training 1,300+ athletes, this system provides simple, instant, real-time feedback inside the training environment, during the movement.

How It Works

Instead of relying on distracting screens, mobile apps, or delayed analysis, the Kaz Path System introduces a visual reference directly into the rack/platform area.

  • Simple & Instant: Immediate understanding of correct vs. incorrect movement.
  • Integrated: A practical coaching tool built into standard equipment.
  • Effective: Improves focus by reducing overthinking during the session.

The Lights

  • Star Lift — an exceptional, textbook rep.
  • Good Lift — clean and correct.
  • Not Bad — close, minor correction needed.
  • Wrong — technical error, adjust immediately.

Why You Need It

  • Fix Errors Instantly: Stops pushing forward or cutting your pull short.
  • Accelerate Learning: Builds perfect muscle memory in half the time.
  • Build Confidence: Gives you a physical, visual target to chase.

How to Try It Now

  1. Step into the rack.
  2. Keep the bar inside the vertical corridor.
  3. Hit the horizontal ceiling at the top.

Who It's For

From beginner to elite, from high-performance training centers to school youth programs, the Kaz Path System is designed to improve how technique is taught and learned. The goal isn't to replace coaching — it's to make feedback:

✓ Instant ✓ Clear ✓ Consistent ✓ Easy to Understand
